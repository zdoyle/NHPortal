eVIN Mismatch Trigger Calculations and Presentation

Calculations

Trigger results are computed by first comparing the eVIN (when reported*) for a tested vehicle as part of their OBDII inspection to the VIN entered at the beginning of the inspection procedure. The results of this comparison are used to calculate a “no-match” rate in % for each inspection station between reported eVINs and the entered VINs.

* If the OBD VIN field in an inspection is NULL and the model year is equal to or greater than 2005, then the VID data will be searched for a record matching the entered VIN. If a matching record is found with a reported OBD VIN, the record with the NULL OBD VIN field will be included in the calculation for eVIN triggers .

Trigger index scores are then calculated for each station by using an algorithm that compares the station’s performance to that of other stations in the inspection network, as shown below:

            Index score = [(highest no-match rate – station rate) / highest rate] x 100

 Using this algorithm, stations that have a 0% no-match rate would have a score of 100, which reflects a perfect record of properly entered VINs.   The station(s) with the highest no-match rate would have a score of 0, which reflects the worst degree of improperly entered VINs (indicating a high likelihood of clean scanning) across the entire network.   Other stations will be distributed along the 0-100 index scale, with lower scores reflecting a greater incidence of suspected clean scanning.

There is an additional option available in the calculation of this trigger. The user has the ability to calculate the trigger % only when the reported VIN passes a check digit check. Limiting the trigger results by this option is available to the user by checking the ‘Omit Invalid OBD VINs’ box that is located on the Initial Data Entry screen. The default method for calculation of the eVIN trigger will be to include all reported OBD VINs regardless of the check digit accuracy.

Presentation

Trigger results are grouped in deciles based on their index scores (0 = 0 to less than 10, 1 = 10 to less than 20, …, 9 = 90 to 100) for ease of presentation.  Users can view the decile results in several forms (pie, bar, line, area or spline).   Those deciles that do not contain any results (e.g., decile 3 if no stations had index scores of 30 to less than 40) are not displayed.  

Viewing the graphic results in bar chart form allows a user to quickly see the overall distribution of trigger results throughout the network.   For example, a bar chart that shows almost all stations in the ninth decile reflects very good (proper) network performance on that trigger.   One with a substantial fraction of stations in the lower deciles may be evidence of wider-spread performance problems.   However, this would not be true if the worst no-match rate is actually very low.

User can also click on the individual bars to see a listing of trigger results for each of the stations making up that bar.   This brings up a table that shows the underlying data—total test count and number of matches, no matches and unknowns (the latter reflects those vehicles which do not have a reported EVIN)—as well as the computed match, no-match and unknown rates for each station.   The table contents can be sorted by any of the column headings by clicking on that heading. 

Because of the small number of stations in some of the deciles, it is not possible to click on those bars to bring up the list of stations.   Users can instead display the results in pie chart form and click on either the applicable slice or the title of the slice.   This allows a user to bring up the station results for all deciles displayed in the charts.

Index Score Details

After clicking on the chart bar (or slice, if pie chart is used) to open a decile, the user is presented with the list of the stations in that decile. On the left side of that list will be a red “Details” box for each station. To view a station’s details click on the red “Details” button. This will present the Detail Trigger IQ section where you can determine the level of detail you want to retrieve by checking the Info boxes at the top of the screen.

The detailed info that will be retrieved depends on which Info boxes you check. Trigger information will always be displayed regardless of which Info boxes you check or if you don’t check any of the Info boxes.

The detail inspection results returned for eVIN triggers should be interpreted this way:

When a mismatch between the entered VIN and the OBD VIN exists; the entered VIN field will be RED with white font and the OBD VIN field will be BLUE with white font. In a case where the OBD VIN field is NULL and the model year is equal to or greater than 2005 and an OBD VIN was reported in another inspection, the blank eVIN field will be BLUE and the Model Year field will be RED with white font. Records marked with these colored fields indicate possible clean scanned inspections based upon eVIN reporting.
